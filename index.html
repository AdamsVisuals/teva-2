Our Story

Founded in 2015 and headquartered in Arusha, Tanzania, Teva Eco Travel is East Africa's leading sustainable safari and eco-tourism company. Our mission is to provide transformative travel experiences while preserving Tanzania's natural beauty and supporting local communities. Our vision is to become the global standard for sustainable African tourism that benefits wildlife, ecosystems, and local communities.

Our team of expert guides and hospitality professionals share a passion for preserving natural wonders while creating unforgettable experiences for our guests. Each journey is designed to foster deep connections with people, wildlife and landscapes.

Our Commitment

Sustainability & Conservation Initiatives

At Teva Eco Travel, we don't just talk about sustainability - we live it. These are the concrete actions we're taking to protect the ecosystems we love.

Serengeti Rhino Protection Program

Partnership with Tanzania Wildlife Authority, anti-poaching patrol support, habitat restoration, and community education programs have achieved a 27% increase in black rhino population since 2018.

Maasai Community Development

Educational scholarships, sustainable income opportunities, women's entrepreneurship programs, healthcare initiatives, and traditional knowledge preservation projects are empowering the Maasai community.

Zanzibar Marine Conservation

Coral reef restoration, sustainable fishing education, plastic pollution reduction, marine protected area development, and sea turtle conservation are safeguarding Zanzibar's marine ecosystems.

Kilimanjaro Porter Welfare

Fair wages, proper equipment, educational opportunities, healthcare benefits, and career advancement pathways ensure the well-being of Kilimanjaro porters and guides.

Carbon Offsetting Program

Comprehensive carbon calculation, local reforestation projects, renewable energy initiatives, emissions reduction strategies, and transparent reporting are reducing our carbon footprint.

Wildlife Expertise

Wildlife Viewing Information

Discover the best locations and times to witness Tanzania's spectacular wildlife with our expert guides.

Lion

Best Viewing: Central Serengeti and Ngorongoro Crater

Behavior: Most active at dawn and dusk, often seen in prides

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Leopard

Best Viewing: Central Serengeti and Tarangire

Behavior: Solitary, often resting in trees during the day

Conservation Status: Vulnerable

Elephant

Best Viewing: Tarangire and Northern Serengeti

Behavior: Large herds, especially near water sources in dry season

Conservation Status: Endangered

Rhino

Best Viewing: Ngorongoro Crater (Black Rhino)

Behavior: Solitary, most active at night and early morning

Conservation Status: Critically Endangered

Buffalo

Best Viewing: Throughout Serengeti and Ngorongoro

Behavior: Large herds, often seen near water sources

Conservation Status: Least Concern

June - July

Northern Serengeti

River Crossings Begin

First dramatic crossings of the Grumeti and Mara Rivers as herds head to Kenya.

August - October

Mara River Crossings

The most spectacular crossings occur in northern Serengeti with crocodile action.

November

Return South

Herds begin moving back through eastern Serengeti as the short rains begin.

Lighting Conditions

  • Golden hours: First and last 2 hours of daylight
  • Midday: Best for waterhole shots and silhouettes
  • Overcast days: Ideal for even lighting and colors

Recommended Equipment

  • DSLR or mirrorless with 200-400mm lens
  • Bean bag for vehicle window support
  • Extra batteries and memory cards
  • Polarizing filter for reducing glare

Specialized Vehicles

  • Pop-top roofs for 360Â° shooting
  • Camera mounts and stabilizing equipment
  • Charging stations for equipment
  • Expert driver/guides who understand photographers' needs

Hiding Techniques

  • Use vehicle as a blind (animals are accustomed)
  • Wear neutral-colored clothing
  • Move slowly and avoid sudden movements
  • Position downwind from your subject

Ethical Guidelines

  • Never bait or harass animals for photos
  • Maintain safe distances (use zoom lenses)
  • Avoid flash photography with nocturnal animals
  • Respect restricted areas and breeding grounds
